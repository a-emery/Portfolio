About Me

My adventure into software began with front-end work, but has since evolved into a more holistic view of the development process.

After a three year stint in teaching middle and high school, I attended a three month, front-end boot camp through The Iron Yard. This experience set me up for my first gig as a developer: a short-term contract working on demo products for a large benefits company.

My real development as an engineer came through my next position. What started as a purely JavaScript based front-end job morphed into a fast-paced learning of databases, then some basic Python, and eventually developing applications using Django. This experience reshaped my view of the product life-cycle and pushed me towards embracing full-stack development.
